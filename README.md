## Which Location Should You Pick?

This is the question that actually matters, and the answer is almost always "whichever one is geographically closest to your main audience."

**USA (Spokane)** is the best default if your users are mostly in North America. It also has the most bandwidth per dollar — the L8G gives you 12TB/month, which is genuinely generous.

**UK (Coventry)** is the obvious pick for European traffic. Layer runs a proper European data center here, which matters both for latency and, depending on your use case, for data sovereignty considerations.

**Singapore** is the one to pick if you're serving Southeast Asia, East Asia, or Australia. The Singapore node has shown strong benchmark numbers from the community — 4k random read IOPS clocking in at 64k+ in tests, network speeds consistently hitting gigabit-level throughput in multiple regions. The bandwidth ceiling is lower than USA/UK at the same price, but the latency advantage for Asian audiences more than makes up for it.

## Is There a Catch?

A few things worth knowing before you sign up:

**Resources are shared, not dedicated.** Like all VPS providers at this price point, the CPU and storage resources are on a fair-share basis, not exclusively allocated to you. In practice this rarely matters for most workloads, but it's not a bare-metal server.

**No Windows.** Layer's platform currently doesn't support Windows server installs. If you need Windows, you'll need to look elsewhere.

**Port 25 is blocked by default.** Standard stuff for any hosting provider trying to avoid spam abuse. If you need to send email from your server, you'll want a dedicated outbound mail service like SendGrid or Mailgun.

**No IRC, TOR, public proxies, or crypto mining.** Legal services are welcome; the usual prohibited list applies.

**Payments.** PayPal is one confirmed payment method for their VPS services.
